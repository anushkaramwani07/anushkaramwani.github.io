In 1949, Anni Albers became the first textile designer to have a solo exhibition at the Museum of Modern Art in New York. Anni Albers was a textile artist born Annelise Elsa Frieda Fleischmann on June 12,1899, in Berlin, Germany. The school moved to Dessau in 1926, and a new focus on production rather than craft at the Bauhaus prompted Anni Albers to develop many functionally unique textiles combining properties of light reflection, sound absorption, durability, and minimized wrinkling and warping tendencies. Anni Albers, who was Jewish, made the move with her husband and the Bauhaus to Berlin, but then fled to North Carolina, where the couple was invited by Philip Johnson to teach at the experimental Black Mountain College, arriving stateside in November 1933.
